Preventive maintenance for home appliances

Simple maintenance routines that extend the life of your appliances, save energy, and prevent costly repairs. Follow these tips and your appliances will last years longer.

Washing Machine
Recommended lifespan: 10-14 years
Monthly
  • Run a cleaning cycle with washer cleaner or white vinegar
  • Wipe down the door seal and remove lint buildup
  • Clean the detergent drawer with warm water
  • Leave the door open after each use to prevent mold
Every 3-6 months
  • Check and clean the drain pump filter
  • Inspect hoses for cracks or bulging
  • Check that the machine is level
  • Clean under and behind the machine
Yearly
  • Replace inlet hoses if older than 5 years
  • Check water pressure — too high can damage valves
  • Inspect the drum for rust or damage
Pro tip: Always use HE (High Efficiency) detergent in front-load washers. Regular detergent creates too many suds and can damage the drum bearings over time.

Dryer
Recommended lifespan: 10-13 years
Every use
  • Clean the lint trap before every cycle
  • Never run the dryer without the lint screen
  • Don't overload — clothes need room to tumble
Every 3 months
  • Clean the lint trap slot with a vacuum
  • Inspect and clean the exhaust duct
  • Check the exterior vent flap opens properly
  • Wipe the drum interior with a damp cloth
Yearly
  • Professional duct cleaning — prevents fire risk
  • Check and clean the moisture sensor bars
  • Inspect the drum belt for wear
Warning: A clogged dryer vent is the #1 cause of home dryer fires in the US. If your dryer takes more than one cycle to dry clothes, the vent is likely clogged — call us right away.

Refrigerator
Recommended lifespan: 13-17 years
Monthly
  • Clean door gaskets with warm soapy water
  • Check that door seals are airtight (paper test)
  • Clean the drip pan under the fridge
  • Keep temperature between 35-38°F
Every 6 months
  • Vacuum the condenser coils (back or bottom)
  • Replace the water filter if you have an ice maker
  • Clean the ice maker and water dispenser
  • Pull out fridge and clean behind it
Yearly
  • Inspect door hinges and adjust if needed
  • Check the defrost drain for clogs
  • Verify the freezer temperature is 0°F
Pro tip: Dirty condenser coils are the #1 cause of refrigerator failure in Texas. The heat and dust in the DFW area make coils get dirty faster — clean them every 6 months.

Stove / Oven
Recommended lifespan: 13-15 years
After every use
  • Wipe down burners and stovetop when cool
  • Clean spills immediately to prevent baking on
  • Replace drip pans when stained or warped
Monthly
  • Deep clean burner grates and caps
  • Run oven self-clean cycle (if available)
  • Clean the oven door glass inside and out
  • Check gas burner ignitors for proper spark
Yearly
  • Check gas connections for leaks (soap test)
  • Calibrate oven temperature with a thermometer
  • Inspect oven door hinges and seal
  • Clean the exhaust hood filters
Safety tip: If you smell gas near your stove, don't turn on any switches. Leave the house immediately and call your gas company. Never try to fix a gas leak yourself.

Dishwasher
Recommended lifespan: 9-12 years
Weekly
  • Scrape food off dishes before loading
  • Check and clean the spray arms
  • Wipe the door edges and gasket
  • Use the right amount of detergent
Monthly
  • Clean and rinse the filter at the bottom
  • Run a cleaning cycle with white vinegar
  • Clean the drain to prevent clogs
  • Check the door latch for proper closing
Yearly
  • Inspect the door gasket for cracks or wear
  • Check water inlet valve for mineral buildup
  • Run a descaling cycle — important in Dallas hard water areas
Dallas tip: The DFW area has very hard water. Use a rinse aid every cycle and run a descaling treatment every 3 months to prevent mineral buildup in your dishwasher and extend its life significantly.
